BOOTCAMP

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway:

I want to get straight into working as a developer in the industry. I want to be an experienced devleoper, who can be counted on, not only in tech, but also in being there as a team member. I want people to feel comfortable enough to come to me with their problems, and trust me to push them to be there best.
And further down the track, on the tech side, I want to work in the science based research on wildlife and their habitats. I want to something that is meaningful, that it creating something to help others, and mainly helping animals.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp:

I am extremely motivated, and have been self-driven in my studies for many years now. Working in tech, is a big driver for me, and so it will help me push through those more difficult times. I love working in team environments, and doing so really aids in my learning. I am an extremely visual learner, and so can learn super fast if I am able to use this to my advantage. On the other-hand, I get overwhelmed with a lot of theory and text, which makes me learn a lot slower, and I can get overwhelmed and self isolate.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be:

Staying on top of my work will be one of my main challenges. And keeping that motivation going for 9 weeks. Also making sure I don't just agree to things when working with a team, and actually stating my opinion.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy:

I would like to work on my confidence in group projects, not being scare to put my ideas out there. And learning to not care too much about what others think about where I am in my learning with tech. As everyone has different ways of learning.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team:

To support me in my learning needs, in the way that I need visual and active demonstrations of things. This is how I learn, and with this technique I can actually learn fast. If I am just given a set of instructions, it takes me a lot longer. To encourage me to make mistakes, as that's a part of learning that I do tend to forget sometimes. And to push my to speak my mind in group projects, not just listening to everyone elses.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp:

To study hard, and push myself to continue my learning once 'class' is over, in order to maximise my learning from this course. To approach everything with determination. To take myself on walks, spend time with friends every once in a while, and in general take care of myself. As I have learnt that forcing myself to study for hours on end,is not going to help me learn. I need to go to rehab for my back, and make sure I'm extremely careful with how long I am sitting so I don't get any flare ups. Overall give this course my absolute full attention, and try my hardest in all aspects.
